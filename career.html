Career Pathways

My career path may evolve, but two distinct routes are clear after completing my Master’s degree:

1. Becoming a University Professor

In this path, I aim to contribute to both research and education by pursuing a PhD and eventually securing a faculty position. The key steps are as follows:

  • 2025: Begin my research-based Master’s degree, focusing on deepening my expertise in data science and its applications. This will also be a time for exploring potential PhD pathways and identifying areas of interest for future research.
  • 2027: Complete my Master’s degree and start pursuing a PhD in data science or a related field. My focus will be on contributing to cutting-edge research, publishing papers, and collaborating with both academic and industry partners.
  • 2027 - 2032: Continue advancing my research during my PhD, concentrating on impactful problems in my chosen field Alongside research, I will begin teaching responsibilities to develop my skills in education and mentorship.
  • 2032: Complete my PhD and secure a postdoctoral or assistant professor position. At this stage, I will focus on building a strong research portfolio, applying for grants, and mentoring students. My long-term goal is to become a thought leader in my field, contributing to the advancement of data science in both academia and industry.

2. Transitioning into Product Management

Alternatively, I could transition into product management, using data-driven insights to guide strategic product decisions at scale. The steps for this path are:

  • 2025: Start my Master’s degree, focusing on expanding my technical knowledge and exploring areas of interest within data science and business intelligence.
  • 2027: Graduate with a Master’s degree and begin working as a Junior Data Scientist, ideally in a research or industry role. Alongside, I will continue learning about product management through formal courses or self-study.
  • 2027: Transition into a Senior Data Scientist role, gaining experience and technical expertise. During this time, I will focus on acquiring the skills needed to move into product management.
  • 2032: Secure a Product Manager position, utilizing data science insights to drive product strategy and development. My goal is to make data-driven decisions that lead to innovation and impact.
